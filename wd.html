Week 1 : Introduction to ID2116 and the micro:bit.

17.01.2020

With prior knowledge of JavaScript from an introductory class from Codecademy, I went for my first class in ID2116 with confidence. I knew next to nothing about computing and knew that I could be slow to pick up on things, so I was still a bit nervous to begin. "What if the class moves too fast and I get left behind again?" "What if, by the end of today, I am completely confused and I don't even know what I don't know?" I was really afraid. But thankfully it was alright!
The class begin with an intoduction of the module before moving on to the micro:bit. Thank goodness I watched a video about it the night before! The micro:bit is an amazing tiny thing that can accomplish so much - and I bet it is capable of more fascinating things when we mix its capabilities with our creativity.
LEARNING TAKEAWAYS

  • A micro:bit is capable of...
    • shaking;
    • turning;
    • pressing (buttons);
    • responding via displaying LED formations in terms of letters or shapes.
  • Creating a set of instructions using the online weblink.
  • Translating my set of instructions into actual actions to-be-performed by the micro:bit.

I'm quite amazed by it because I know that there are so much unexplored domains waiting to be discovered. I wonder, what else is the micro:bit capable of?
